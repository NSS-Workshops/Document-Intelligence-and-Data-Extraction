Documents generally fall into three types: structured, semi-structred, and unstructured.

---

## Structured

Structured documents have a fixed schema and predictable format. Data appear in consistent locations and follows defined field types.

Examples:
* csv files
* spreadsheets
* databases

---

## Semi-Structured

Semi-structured documents have a consistent layout but flexible formatting. Certain values are expected to appear, but their exact location, labeling, or formatting may vary between documents.

Examples:
* invoices
* receipts
* shipping labels
* forms

---

## Unstructured

Unstructured documents have no fixed layout or predefined schema. Information is embedded within free-form text.

Examples:
* emails
* contracts
* reports
* letters


## Document Intelligence and Document Types

The type of document strongly influences the difficulty of extraction.  
* Structured documents require little to no extraction since fields are already organized.  
* Semi-structured documents present moderate challenges due to layout and formatting variability.  
* Unstructured documents are the most difficult, often requiring interpretation of paragraphs of text that may span multiple pages.

As documents become less strucutred, more advanced techniques such as OCR and natural language processing are often required.
